      "summary_comment": "This paper introduces Soft Weight Rescaling (SWR), a simple per-layer norm-based rescaling that preserves model outputs up to a scalar by jointly scaling biases, aiming to prevent unbounded weight growth and restore plasticity. The method is easy to apply and shows promising empirical gains in warm-start, continual learning, and standard training, particularly for larger models. Theoretical sections provide intuitive arguments for proportionality, norm boundedness, and improved layer balancedness.\n\nHowever, the novelty over prior rescaling and normalization literature is limited, and the theoretical results depend on strong assumptions or are known in similar forms. The empirical study, while covering multiple settings, lacks comparisons to stronger baselines (e.g., spectral norm regularization, Path-SGD, SAM) and more thorough ablations, leaving open questions about robustness and generality. Moreover, clarity issues and incomplete training details impede reproducibility, and some unusually large gains with learning-rate decay merit deeper analysis. Overall, the work is interesting and practical but not yet sufficiently mature in positioning, rigor, and evaluation for acceptance."

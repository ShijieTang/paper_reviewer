      "summary_comment": "This paper proposes PAPRIKA, a post-training method to instill general information-seeking and sequential decision-making capabilities in LLMs through self-generated multi-turn trajectories and multi-turn preference optimization (RPO). The work is well-presented, pragmatic, and comprehensive: it defines a suite of ten interactive tasks, shows cross-group generalization (LOO), includes ablations indicating the value of preference learning, and introduces a simple UCB-based curriculum using coefficient of variation to improve sampling efficiency. Results on two base models show robust gains in success rates and reduced turns-to-solve without harming standard benchmarks. The main concerns are the reliance on LLM-driven environments and judges (susceptible to residual hacking), the toyish nature of most tasks, modest curriculum gains with heuristic justification, and limited comparison to alternative training paradigms. Despite these caveats, the empirical evidence and released resources make this a valuable contribution toward scalable, amortized exploration for LLM agents."

      "summary_comment": "This paper proposes OT-CP, a multivariate conformal prediction method that transforms vector-valued scores to a uniform spherical target via an optimal transport map and then applies conformal prediction to the norm of the transformed scores. The idea is appealing and leverages center-outward ranks to yield better-calibrated scalarizations than simple norms, and the empirical results across a multi-output benchmark suggest improved region sizes over common baselines. The implementation with entropic OT and ablations on grid size and regularization are useful. However, the theoretical exposition has gaps: the coverage guarantee hinges on exchangeability that is not clearly preserved unless a split or fully permutation-invariant procedure is used; this is not precisely specified. The presentation quality is weak, with multiple typos, undefined symbols, and broken references, and the evaluation lacks sufficient procedural detail and broader baselines. The computational burden is significant and warrants deeper discussion. Overall, while the concept is promising, the paper needs clearer, more rigorous treatment of the coverage mechanism and a cleaner, more comprehensive empirical evaluation before acceptance."

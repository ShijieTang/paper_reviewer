      "summary_comment": "This paper studies why diffusion models often avoid memorizing training data and proposes an implicit dynamical regularization mechanism arising from training dynamics. Empirically, the authors identify two timescales: τgen (when high-quality generation begins) and τmem (when memorization appears), with τgen roughly constant and τmem growing linearly with dataset size n and inversely with model width W. Theoretical analysis in a random-features framework explains these findings via a two-bulk spectrum of a feature correlation matrix, tying relaxation times to eigenvalues and demonstrating why an expanding early-stopping window prevents memorization even in overparameterized regimes. The work is clearly written, well-situated within related literature, provides code, and reports uncertainty. While experiments are limited to a single low-resolution dataset and use a memorization metric in pixel space, and the theoretical setting simplifies real training, the combination of empirical scaling laws and rigorous analysis offers meaningful insight and practical guidance. I recommend acceptance."

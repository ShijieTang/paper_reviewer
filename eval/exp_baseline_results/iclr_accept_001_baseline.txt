The key idea is to learn a compact latent space for short motion primitives via a VAE and to generate sequences by autoregressively denoising latent variables conditioned on history and text. The same latent space supports spatial control through gradient-based latent noise optimization and RL policies, enabling tasks like in-betweening, scene-aware interactions, and goal-reaching. Experiments show strong motion realism, efficient generation (>300 fps), human preference advantages, and improved controllability, with comprehensive ablations supporting the design. Main concerns are reliance on frame-aligned text labels, limited quantitative evaluation for scene interactions, kinematic artifacts that may require physics post-processing, and some clarity/formatting issues.

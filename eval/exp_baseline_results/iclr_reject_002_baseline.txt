      "summary_comment": "The paper proposes a one-step sampling approach for flow matching: compute source–target pairs by integrating an explicit flow-matching velocity backward from target samples to “prototypes” in the base distribution, then train a direct map from prototypes to targets. The idea of using an explicit analytical velocity to induce couplings for a deterministic generator is interesting and could offer inference-time speedups. The algorithmic description is clear, includes a conditional extension, and the role of σ for invertibility is well-motivated with toy experiments.\n\nHowever, the work falls short on empirical rigor and demonstrated impact. Experiments are limited to small-scale datasets with only qualitative visuals; there are no quantitative metrics, baseline comparisons, or speed measurements, making it difficult to assess benefits relative to established straightening and distillation methods. Theoretical claims are mostly heuristic, and important aspects (bias from self-normalized IS, stability and accuracy of backward integration, guarantees that the learned map matches the target distribution) are not addressed. Clarity is acceptable but marred by notation and formatting issues. Overall, while the concept is promising, the current submission lacks the depth and evidence needed for acceptance."

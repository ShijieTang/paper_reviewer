      "summary_comment": "This paper refines the link between transformer attention and dense associative memory by introducing an unsupervised in-context denoising task. The authors derive Bayes-optimal estimators for subspaces, spheres, and small-noise Gaussian mixtures, and show that single-layer transformers (linear or softmax attention) can achieve these solutions, with training recovering scaled identity weights. A central contribution is the mechanistic mapping from trained attention to a one-step gradient descent update on a DAM energy landscape, explaining why single-step updates can outperform exact retrieval under denoising. Theoretical results are sound and supported by synthetic experiments, including convergence with context length and loss landscape analyses. The main limitations are the restricted scope to simple distributions and toy settings, and the reliance on symmetry/identity-like weights, leaving generalization to richer tasks and multi-layer architectures as future work. Overall, the work offers a meaningful conceptual advance and solid empirical backing, warranting acceptance."

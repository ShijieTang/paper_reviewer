      "summary_comment": "This paper proposes OT-CP, a principled approach to multivariate conformal prediction by mapping vector-valued scores through an optimal transport (center-outward) map to the unit ball and using the radius as a scalar conformity score. The key theoretical contribution is a finite-sample coverage guarantee that holds even with approximate transport maps by calibrating the radius under the empirical pushforward, sidestepping the usual pitfalls of estimating multivariate CDFs or copulas. The implementation via entropic OT is practical, and ablations provide useful guidance. Empirical results on a large benchmark suggest improved region sizes with maintained coverage against standard multivariate CP baselines. However, the exposition has several rough edges (proof clarity, exchangeability assumptions, formatting), and the experimental section would benefit from additional baselines, more systematic numerical reporting, and deeper discussion of computational scalability and set representation. Overall, the contribution is technically sound, conceptually elegant, and practically relevant, meriting acceptance after revisions for clarity and completeness."

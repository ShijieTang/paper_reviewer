However, the paper suffers from notable issues: (1) a questionable claim of an unbiased intersection estimator obtained by multiplying two unbiased estimators, without addressing dependence or bias correction; (2) significant clarity problems and inconsistent notation in definitions and attention formulations; (3) missing ablations and fairness checks (e.g., comparing within the same architecture to standard encodings, reporting sketch hyperparameters, and measuring runtime/memory overhead); and (4) limited theoretical depth beyond a straightforward invariance result.

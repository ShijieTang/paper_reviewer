      "summary_comment": "This paper develops a principled theory of gradient-based training on analog in-memory hardware under generic asymmetric and non-linear device response functions. It shows Analog SGD implicitly optimizes a penalized objective that draws weights toward device-specific symmetric points, causing inexact convergence. The proposed Residual Learning framework, cast as a bilevel problem, aligns the algorithmic stationary and physical symmetric points and achieves provable convergence under stated assumptions; extensions address IO noise and granularity. The analysis is rigorous and the empirical results, while simulation-based and not at very large scale, support the theory and show gains over Analog SGD and competitive performance with Tiki-Taka, especially when asymmetry is pronounced. Some assumptions (strong convexity, zero-shift) and the lack of hardware results limit generality, and broader baselines would strengthen the evaluation. Overall, the paper meaningfully advances the theoretical understanding of analog training dynamics and offers practical guidance, warranting acceptance."

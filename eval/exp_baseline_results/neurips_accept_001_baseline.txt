      "summary_comment": "This paper develops a rigorous theoretical framework for analog in-memory training under generic asymmetric and nonlinear device response functions. It identifies an implicit penalty that biases Analog SGD toward device symmetric points and shows this causes inexact convergence. The proposed Residual Learning scheme, cast as a bilevel optimization with a mixing coefficient, provably mitigates this bias and achieves exact convergence under zero-shifted response conditions; it generalizes beyond the linear settings studied in prior work and explains when Tiki-Taka can fail. Practical extensions (buffering and thresholding) address I/O noise and granularity, with supportive simulation results across several models and datasets. While the theory rests on strong convexity and asymptotic approximations and the experiments are simulations rather than hardware demonstrations, the conceptual advances and analyses are timely and meaningful for the AIMC community.

      "summary_comment": "This paper presents a compelling account of why diffusion models often avoid memorization despite overparameterization, attributing it to an implicit dynamical regularization that creates a widening early-stopping window between two timescales: tau_gen (sample quality) and tau_mem (memorization). The empirical findings—particularly tau_mem ∝ n and tau_gen ≈ constant, along with scaling in model width—are clear and well-supported. The accompanying random-features analysis provides a principled explanation via the spectrum of the training dynamics, recovering two relaxation regimes with distinct dependencies on n and p. The work is timely, theoretically grounded, and practically relevant (early stopping guidance, capacity control). Main limitations concern dataset scope, reliance on an L2 NN memorization metric, and the idealized theoretical model. Nonetheless, the overall contribution is strong and advances understanding of generalization versus memorization in diffusion models."

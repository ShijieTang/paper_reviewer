      "summary_comment": "This paper proposes DO-EM, an EM-like framework for density operator latent variable models, deriving a Quantum ELBO and leveraging a quantum information projection solved by the Petz Recovery Map under a sufficiency condition. The theoretical development is interesting and largely sound under stated assumptions, and the CQ-LVM specialization makes the approach implementable on classical data. The introduction of QiDBMs with tractable Gibbs steps is practically appealing and shows substantial FID gains over DBMs on MNIST-scale experiments. However, the theoretical guarantees hinge on rather restrictive assumptions (separability, commuting marginals, diagonal visibles), limiting the quantum generality. The main large-scale experiments use Contrastive Divergence, so the ascent guarantees do not apply to the practical training regime. Empirical evaluation is confined to MNIST and DBM comparisons with FID, lacking broader baselines and likelihood metrics. Clarity and positioning could also be improved. Overall, while the ideas are promising and novel, the current empirical scope and restrictions of the theory make the work borderline; I recommend rejection in its present form, with potential for a stronger future version that broadens applicability and strengthens evaluation."

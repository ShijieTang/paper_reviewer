      "summary_comment": "This paper develops DO-EM, an EM-style learning framework for density-operator latent variable models. It derives a quantum ELBO using monotonicity of quantum relative entropy and frames the E-step as a quantum information projection, showing that the Petz recovery map solves it under a sufficiency condition. This yields a minorant-maximization procedure with likelihood ascent guarantees for a broad but restricted class of models. The specialization to classical data (CQ-LVMs) enables practical training on classical hardware via block-diagonal structure, and the proposed QiDBM architecture affords closed-form conditional sampling, allowing Contrastive Divergence training with a DBM-like footprint. Empirically, the method shows monotone improvement in a small exact setting and provides sizable FID gains over DBMs on MNIST/Binarized MNIST. The main limitations are the restrictive conditions underpinning the theoretical guarantees and the relatively narrow experimental scope and baselines. Despite these, the theoretical framing is novel and well grounded, and the empirical results are promising. I recommend acceptance."

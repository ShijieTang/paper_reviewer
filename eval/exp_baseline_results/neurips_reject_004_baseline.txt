      "summary_comment": "This paper proposes TDFormer, a spiking Transformer augmented with a biologically inspired top-down feedback pathway that explicitly connects time steps, aiming to improve temporal information integration and gradient flow. The approach is well-motivated and technically instantiated via a control module that fuses top-down and bottom-up signals for attention and a processing module with a clamped attention mechanism that admits a variance bound. Empirically, the method consistently improves over strong baselines across multiple static and neuromorphic datasets and achieves a new SNN SOTA on ImageNet with minimal overhead. Theoretical analyses provide useful intuition but rely on simplifying assumptions and include claims (e.g., non-zero gradients) that would benefit from more precise conditions and bounds. The large-scale evaluation, while extensive, would be stronger with stricter fairness controls and hardware-based efficiency metrics. Despite these concerns, the contributions are novel and impactful for the SNN community, and the empirical gains are substantial and well-supported, warranting acceptance."

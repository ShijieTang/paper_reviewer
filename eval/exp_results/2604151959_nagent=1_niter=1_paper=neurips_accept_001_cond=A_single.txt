      "summary_comment": "This work delivers a strong, novel, and impactful theoretical foundation for analog in-memory training under generic non-ideal response functions. It identifies and formalizes an implicit penalty induced by asymmetric responses, proving that Analog SGD converges inexactly with a non-vanishing asymptotic error, and introduces a Residual Learning framework—interpreted as a bilevel optimization—that provably attains exact convergence when the hardware response is zero-shifted around the origin. The theory explains the limitations of prior Tiki-Taka results in non-ideal settings and justifies the benefit of non-zero mixing. Beyond theory, the paper extends the method to address IO noise and update granularity and validates claims with extensive AIHWKIT simulations on MNIST and CIFAR10/100 with modern architectures. While some assumptions (strong convexity, zero-shift feasibility) and the lack of hardware experiments limit the scope, the core ideas are creative, forward-looking, and likely to influence both algorithm and device co-design. Given the strong novelty and significance with acceptable soundness and solid empirical support, I recommend acceptance."
